Recommendations
Prioritized actions based on verified Hubble data (July 14, 2026)
1
Smart Disputes — Go-Live Imminent
Amendment signed Jul 3
Win rates of 3.9% (Joist) and 4.9% (Invoice Simple) mean nearly every dispute becomes a permanent loss. Smart Disputes automates evidence collection and submission. Phase 1 eligibility covers roughly 25% of disputes; estimated first-year recovery $100–200K, scaling in Phases 2 and 3. Opt-out rollout starts with Joist and Invoice Simple connected accounts.
2
Investigate Joist "Debit Not Authorized" Large-Dollar Fraud
Immediate
14 disputes, $369K in losses, $28K average per dispute — 10x the average fraudulent dispute. This pattern is consistent with synthetic identity or business impersonation targeting large single-transaction amounts. Pull the specific connected accounts generating these disputes and cross-reference against weekly fraud submissions to determine if they overlap with known bad actors or represent a new vector.
3
Bank Account Verification for New Joist CAs
Priority Discussion
The debit-only payout fraud pattern is confirmed by both Stripe data and EC's independent reviews. Requiring bank account ownership verification before enabling payouts on new Joist connected accounts would disrupt this vector at onboarding. This capability is already active on the platform. Discussion item: make it mandatory for new Joist CAs.
4
Identity Verification for High-Risk Onboarding
Exploring
The card cashing pattern (fake businesses, AI-generated websites, debit-only payouts) could be intercepted at onboarding with identity verification before account activation. Internal product discussion in progress with EC's priority attached. Timeline update coming within the next few weeks.
5
Platform-Level Fraud Rules
Early Stage
Detect patterns across connected accounts (same device fingerprints, repeated fraud vectors) before disputes occur. Under evaluation for managed risk platforms. No committed timeline yet. We'll update as the product path develops.
